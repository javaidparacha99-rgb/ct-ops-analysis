# Canadian Tire Corporation Operations Analysis (2026)

An operations and performance analysis of Canadian Tire Corporation (TSX: CTC), one of Canada's largest and most recognized retail companies. This project examines publicly reported financial and operational data across all four quarters of 2024 to identify performance trends, segment strengths, and areas for strategic improvement.

---

## What This Project Does

Canadian Tire reports detailed operational and financial data every quarter as a publicly traded company. This project takes that data, structures it, and analyzes it the way an operations or business analyst would — looking beyond the headline numbers to understand what actually drove performance up or down throughout the year.

The output is a fully structured Excel workbook with five sheets covering raw data, KPIs, quarterly trends, segment breakdowns, and written findings with recommendations.

---

## Company Background

Canadian Tire Corporation (TSX: CTC) was founded in 1922 and is one of Canada's most iconic retail brands. It operates through three main segments — Retail, Financial Services, and CT REIT — and runs banners including Canadian Tire Retail, Mark's, SportChek, and Gas+. As of year-end 2024, the company operated 502 stores across 23.1 million square feet of retail space nationwide.

## Key Findings

- Q2 2024 was the weakest quarter with comparable sales down 4.6%, driven by consumer pullback on discretionary spending
- Q4 2024 saw a return to comparable sales growth of 1.1%, led by Automotive and a strong December
- The Triangle Rewards loyalty program accounted for 54.4% of all full-year retail sales — Canadians redeemed $360M in eCTM, up 7%
- Gross margin improved 50 basis points to 36.0% despite a 1.8% revenue decline, showing stronger selling discipline
- Year-end inventory was down 5% vs 2023, representing a meaningful supply chain improvement following the 2023 Brampton DC fire
- Full-year normalized EPS grew 21.7% to $12.62, with Q4 alone up 20.4%

---

## Key Recommendations

1. Increase investment in Automotive and essential categories — the most resilient segments in a cautious consumer environment
2. Deepen Triangle Rewards personalization to reactivate discretionary spending among lapsed members
3. Build better demand sensing tools for Q2, the most volatile quarter due to its discretionary product mix
4. Use the Brampton DC fire recovery as a blueprint for supply chain resilience and redundancy planning
5. Monitor Financial Services credit quality closely as a leading indicator of broader consumer financial stress
